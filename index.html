Two modes

Personal or professional —
you choose the tone.

One toggle changes everything. Your coach adjusts its approach — every read, every suggestion, every practice session — to match the context you're actually in.

Personal mode

Warm and real

Casual, warm, and real. For the people and moments that don't need a professional layer. Your coach steps back and lets you just be yourself — with a little support when you want it.

"Hey, totally fair — I should've looped you in earlier. Let's sync before the call so we're on the same page."
Business mode

Polished and poised

Composed, clear, and professional. For the moments where how you say something matters as much as what you say. Your coach helps you navigate workplace dynamics with confidence.

"Thank you for flagging this. I appreciate the context and will ensure I align with you before moving forward on decisions of this nature."

Skill training

Practice the conversations
that actually matter.

Your coach walks you through the conversations most people never actually practice. Each module is a structured coaching session — context and framing, then a realistic practice round, then a debrief on what you did well and what to try differently next time.

Low stakes
How to ask someone out
Reading signals, phrasing the ask naturally, and responding gracefully whether the answer is yes or no.
Medium stakes
Setting a work boundary
Calm, professional language for protecting your time and energy — with a colleague or with your manager.
Medium stakes
Giving difficult feedback
Framing, tone, and timing that lands without damaging the relationship or demoralizing the other person.
High stakes
Asking for a raise
Building the case, bringing it up for the first time, and responding to "the budget is tight right now."
Low stakes
Navigating small talk
Starting conversations at events, keeping them going, and exiting gracefully without it feeling awkward.
Medium stakes
Handling passive aggression
De-escalation and clarity for when someone is being indirect — in messages and in meetings.
